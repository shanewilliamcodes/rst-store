# RST — Design Vision

**Who it's for:** parents (mostly 28–45) buying comfort as a small act of love —
for themselves, their partner, their kids. They shop on phones, at night, tired.
They are suspicious of drop-ship junk and allergic to hype.

**What it sells:** one perfected object in four family sizes, with a private
neon signature. Not assortment — conviction.

## The vision, in five sentences

RST should feel like its product: **soft, warm, exact.** A quiet editorial
neutral world — real photography, generous space, one characterful serif —
punctured in precisely one way: small, confident flashes of neon at moments of
meaning (the signature, the selected state, the give-back). Typography does the
luxury work: a true display serif (Fraunces, soft axis) for voice, a clean
grotesk for function, and a strict two-level label system instead of uppercase
everywhere. Every interactive element says exactly what it does, every promise on
the page is backed by a working system, and the color you pick is the color you
preview, the color in your cart, and the color the printer receives — one source
of truth from click to doorstep. Trust is the aesthetic.

## System commitments

- **Type:** Fraunces (variable, SOFT axis up) for display; Geist for UI/body.
  Two label styles total. Body floor 14px.
- **Color:** cream field, ink text, oatmeal surfaces at *two* sanctioned tints,
  neon reserved for signature moments and selected states.
- **Shape:** fully-rounded controls (soft brand, soft corners), square-ish media.
- **Preview:** photorealistic per-color product mockups from the fulfillment
  provider (Printful), never illustration. Honest fallback until keys exist.
- **Copy:** warm but load-bearing. No label a shopper has to decode.
